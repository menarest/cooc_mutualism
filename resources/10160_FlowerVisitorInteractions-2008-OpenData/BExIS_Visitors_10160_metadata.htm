General *
* Title flower visitor interactions 2008
* Abstract Specialisation of flower visitors is compared across grasslands along a gradient of land use intensity. We focus on resource-based mechanisms that may determine flower visiting insect communities in different habitats. We analyze flower visiting rates of pollinators in order to measure possible variation between differently used sites. Moreover, we characterize the degree of specialisation on species and community level (pollinator networks; Blüthgen et al. 2007).

* Detailed description of study design Number of time based repetitions (information from the old BExIS schema): 3Between April and August 2008 we studied the 50 experimental plots in each exploratory, sampling different land use types simultaneously. Some plots were sampled up to four times during the year.All momentarily flowering plant species, an independent estimate of the total number of flower units per species, and all flower visitor-plant interactions during six hours will be recorded on a 600 m² transect around each plot.A ‘flower unit’ is defined as a unit of one or more flowers demanding insects to fly from one unit to another. Insects are caught with a dip net or with help of an exhauster. Only insects sitting directly in the centre of the flowers seeming to feed on pollen or nectar are caught. Insects sitting on the petals are not taken into consideration. (Note: former name of the section was measurements/theory)
